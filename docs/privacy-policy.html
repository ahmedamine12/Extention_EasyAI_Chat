BrowseMate — Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026 · Extension version 2.x

This policy describes how the BrowseMate browser extension (“BrowseMate”, “the extension”) handles information when you use it. BrowseMate is designed so your AI keys and chats stay on your device.

Who provides BrowseMate

BrowseMate is published as a Chromium browser extension. For questions: support@browsemate.com.

What BrowseMate does not do

BrowseMate does not operate a cloud service that stores your prompts or API keys. Chat requests are sent from your browser directly to the AI providers you configure (for example OpenAI, Google Gemini, or Hugging Face), using your own API credentials.

Data stored locally on your device

The extension may store the following using your browser’s extension storage (for example chrome.storage.local):

  • API keys and settings you enter (provider choice, toggles, preferences).
  • Chat-related data needed for session or history features (for example messages), kept on your device unless you clear extension data.
  • Reminder metadata you schedule (title, time, optional note) so alarms can fire locally.

This data stays on your machine unless you uninstall the extension, clear extension data, or export it yourself if the product offers export. BrowseMate does not require an account on our servers.

When you use AI providers

When you send a message or image, your browser connects directly to third-party AI services you enabled (OpenAI, Google, Hugging Face, etc.). Those services process content according to their own terms and privacy policies. You are responsible for your accounts and keys with those providers.

Permissions (why they exist)

  • storage — Save keys, settings, and local chat data.
  • activeTab / tabs / scripting — Inject the chat UI, use page context when you ask (for example screenshot or selection), and deliver reminders where supported.
  • contextMenus — Right-click actions on selected text.
  • alarms / notifications — Scheduled reminders you create.
  • <all_urls> (host permission) — Allow the floating UI and outbound requests to AI provider endpoints you use.

Voice input

If you enable voice input, speech recognition is handled by your browser / operating system as configured by you, not by a BrowseMate server.

Children

BrowseMate is not directed at children under 13. Do not use the extension in violation of your local laws or the policies of your AI providers.

Changes

We may update this policy when the extension changes. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when we do.
